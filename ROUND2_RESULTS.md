# Round 2 Results

Tournament over three design concepts, five variants each, judged by a five-member
panel across two aggregation methods (combined score sum and Borda count). The three
concept champions advanced to a grand final judged by the same panel through five
distinct lenses.

## Grand Final

| Rank | Route | Concept | Combined | Borda |
|------|-------|---------|----------|-------|
| 1 | /lab/r2/brutalist-3/ | Swiss / brutalist data-dense index | 175 | 13 |
| 2 | /lab/r2/nodegraph-3/ | Live systems architecture map | 168 | 9 |
| 3 | /lab/r2/editorial-3/ | Print editorial / magazine spread | 166 | 8 |

## Overall Winner: /lab/r2/brutalist-3/ (Swiss / brutalist data-dense index)

brutalist-3 wins the grand final and is the clean, undisputed champion. Both
aggregation methods agree: it tops the combined score (175, ahead of nodegraph-3 at
168 and editorial-3 at 166) and it tops the Borda count (13, ahead of nodegraph-3 at
9 and editorial-3 at 8). There is no combined-vs-borda disagreement at the top of the
grand final; the same route leads on both axes, and it does so by a wider Borda margin
(13 vs 9) than its combined margin (175 vs 168), which means the panel ranked it first
more consistently than the raw point spread alone suggests.

The decisive reason across judges is that brutalist-3 is the only champion that PROVES
its central claim inside the medium rather than asserting it. Its in-browser
reconciliation demo runs a real deterministic trial-balance vs source-ledger cross-check
on synthetic SPV data (SPV-Beta flagged at delta +750.00, SPV-Delta at 62,300.00
unbooked) and closes on "Human owns the decision", which is exactly the human-in-the-loop
finance-ops automation thesis, demonstrated in the first scan. Around that proof it
stacks the highest-density trust signals of the field: a status ribbon answering a
recruiter's first questions (open to work, Luxembourg, June 2026), a shipped-vs-pattern
honesty filter (7 shipped, 2 proposed), data-derived counts that structurally cannot
drift or lie, a numbered keyboard-navigable index (0-8), a "/" command palette, and a
sticky scroll-synced "you are here" location instrument.

Three of the five grand-final judges named brutalist-3 their winner (the clarity lens,
the hiring-manager lens, and the medium-message lens). The other two lenses split:
the visual-craft judge placed editorial-3 first by a single point, and the originality
judge placed nodegraph-3 first decisively. brutalist-3's strength is that it was never
worse than second on any lens, which is precisely why it wins both aggregations.

## Concept Champions

### Swiss / brutalist data-dense index -> /lab/r2/brutalist-3/

Champion of its concept on both methods. Per-concept combined: brutalist-3 led at 99,
ahead of brutalist-2 (94), brutalist-5 (88), brutalist-1 (82), and brutalist-4 (80).
Per-concept Borda: brutalist-3 led at 13, just ahead of brutalist-2 (12), then
brutalist-5 (9), brutalist-1 (6), and brutalist-4 (5). It beat its four siblings by
combining the most rigorous grid discipline with genuinely load-bearing interaction
(the live reconciliation demo, command palette, keyboard jumps, scroll-sync location
readout) and a functional second color for shipped-vs-pattern status rather than
decoration. brutalist-2 was the closest sibling on Borda but trailed on raw points;
brutalist-4 was the weakest variant on both axes.

### Live systems architecture map -> /lab/r2/nodegraph-3/

Champion of its concept on both methods, in an unusually tight field. Per-concept
combined: nodegraph-3 led at 101, with nodegraph-5 (97), nodegraph-1 and nodegraph-4
(both 96), and nodegraph-2 (95) clustered closely behind, the narrowest concept spread
of the tournament. Per-concept Borda: nodegraph-3 led decisively at 12, with the other
four bunched at 8-9. It won its concept on its single-source-of-truth engineering (the
graph clones the one authored linear DOM by id, so counts are derived and the prior 7/9
drift bug is structurally impossible), cursor-anchored pan/zoom with bezier connectors,
a zero-flash no-JS linear fallback, and a mobile signal-trace that preserves the metaphor.

### Print editorial / magazine spread -> /lab/r2/editorial-3/

Champion of its concept on both methods. Per-concept combined: editorial-3 led at 102
(the highest single per-concept combined score in the tournament), ahead of editorial-4
(97), editorial-5 (93), editorial-1 (91), and editorial-2 (87). Per-concept Borda:
editorial-3 led at 12, with editorial-4 second at 11, then editorial-1 (8), and
editorial-2 and editorial-5 tied (7). It beat its siblings on pure typographic craft:
an enforced 8px baseline rhythm, oldstyle figures applied via font-variant-numeric, a
three-family type system with laddered mono letter-spacing, and crafted editorial
signatures (drop cap, accent-ruled pull-quote, giant "hi." contact watermark) that read
as intent rather than pastiche. editorial-4 was the consistent runner-up sibling.

## Grand-Final Judge Headlines

- **Visual-craft lens:** Winner editorial-3 (35), then brutalist-3 (33), then
  nodegraph-3 (32). The only champion with a real enforced baseline rhythm and correctly
  applied typographic fundamentals throughout; the most disciplined surface.
- **Originality lens:** Winner nodegraph-3 (38), then brutalist-3 (35), then editorial-3
  (31). It invents a category (a CV that boots like a service map) rather than executing
  a known idiom, and does so without sacrificing engineering discipline.
- **Clarity / completeness / a11y / mobile lens:** Winner brutalist-3 (36), then
  nodegraph-3 (35), then editorial-3 (33). Its primary experience, not a fallback, is
  the most scannable, and its instruments reinforce orientation rather than gate content.
- **Hiring-manager / 20-second-trust lens:** Winner brutalist-3 (36), then editorial-3
  (33), then nodegraph-3 (29). The only champion that proves the central automation claim
  inside the first scan rather than asserting it.
- **Medium-reinforces-message lens:** Winner brutalist-3 (35), then nodegraph-3 (34),
  then editorial-3 (34). The automation message literally executes in the medium; the
  shell is the register a controller natively reads.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

### /lab/r2/brutalist-3/ (overall winner)
- Strengths: Proves the finance-ops thesis in-medium via a real client-side
  reconciliation demo; highest-density trust signals (status ribbon, shipped-vs-pattern
  honesty filter, derived counts that cannot lie); strongest scannability with a live
  scroll-sync location instrument, numeric index, keyboard jumps, and command palette;
  thorough mobile reflow and accessibility (aria-pressed, aria-live, role=dialog,
  reduced-motion gating). Never ranked below second on any lens.
- Weaknesses: Monospace, data-dense surface at 11.5-13px can fatigue a non-technical
  reader and reads slightly "engineer-coded"; the giant FIG.00 "T" glyph is decorative
  filler; orange plus green can read busy; no skip-to-content link; loses on pure
  typographic finesse to editorial-3.

### /lab/r2/nodegraph-3/ (runner-up)
- Strengths: Highest originality and the most impressive engineering; true
  single-source-of-truth architecture (panel clones the one authored DOM, counts derived,
  drift structurally impossible); cursor-anchored pan/zoom, bezier wires, overview mode,
  topology prev/next; best-in-class progressive enhancement (zero-flash no-JS linear
  default, skip link, mobile signal-trace, reduced-motion drop to overview).
- Weaknesses: Lowest clarity of the three; the default desktop experience asks the visitor
  to learn an interaction model before extracting a single fact, so substance sits one
  click behind topology; node cards are visually plainer; edge-label placement is heuristic
  and collision-prone; detail panel is aria-modal=false with no focus trap. Highest ceiling,
  highest friction.

### /lab/r2/editorial-3/ (third)
- Strengths: The finest pure craft object; enforced 8px baseline rhythm, oldstyle figures,
  three-family type system, crafted editorial signatures (drop cap, ruled pull-quote, "hi."
  watermark); the most effortlessly legible and broadly likeable read; honest content
  judgment (the fig-vs-note metric split stops category words masquerading as quantified
  metrics; an empty glyph replaced by a load-bearing status panel); clean responsive
  collapse and full reduced-motion gating.
- Weaknesses: The most templated form (the magazine/editorial portfolio is the most common
  premium idiom); the least interactive, with no signature mechanic and no in-page proof;
  automation is narrated and link-out rather than enacted; no skip-to-content link; lowest
  on memorability and on medium-message fit despite the top craft score.
